# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Two co-equal audiences share this app, neither secondary to the other. Internal ops staff — dispatchers, admins, finance, CX, and KYC reviewers — run day-to-day courier operations: creating and tracking orders, dispatching riders, resolving tickets and remarks, reviewing KYC, handling holds, returns, and loss & damage. Vendor/merchant users are the businesses shipping through ParcelMoover; they create orders, track deliveries, and manage their own settlements and payments without needing to contact support. Both groups work in the same product, so a screen built for one audience should never degrade the workflow of the other.

## Product Purpose

Run courier operations end-to-end reliably — pickup through dispatch through delivery through settlement — with minimal errors, holds, or lost parcels. The system exists to cut the manual work and reconciliation burden on staff while giving vendors direct, self-service visibility into their own orders and money, so neither side depends on the other to know what's happening.

## Positioning

A unified operations and vendor portal in one system: internal courier operations and vendor self-service live in the same product instead of being split across separate internal and customer-facing tools.

## Brand Personality

Efficient, precise, trustworthy. The interface should read as dependable infrastructure, not a showcase — this is a tool people rely on dozens of times a day to move parcels and money correctly. Good UX here means good applied psychology: recognition over recall, obvious current state, minimal steps between "what's happening with this parcel" and the answer.

## Anti-references

No specific anti-references named. Default guard: avoid anything that trades clarity or speed for visual flourish — this is a high-volume daily-use operations tool, not a showcase.

## Design Principles

Serving two audiences without compromise: internal ops and vendor self-service share one system, and each surface should feel built for its specific user rather than a shared afterthought.

Trust through precision: this system touches money (COD, settlements) and delivery status, so accuracy and error-prevention outrank visual polish whenever the two are in tension.

Reduce cognitive load: favor recognition over recall, make status and next actions obvious at a glance, and minimize the distance between a question ("where is this parcel / this settlement") and its answer.

Reliability over cleverness: in a tool used at high volume every day, a straightforward, predictable interaction beats a novel one.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG level mandated. Follow standard good practice as a baseline: solid color contrast, full keyboard navigation, and a reduced-motion alternative for any animation.
